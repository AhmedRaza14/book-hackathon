# Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook Specification

## Overview
This textbook aims to bridge the gap between digital AI models and embodied intelligence, focusing on the integration of AI with physical robotics systems. The book will cover both theoretical foundations and practical implementation of humanoid robots capable of intelligent interaction with the physical world.

## Target Audience
- Advanced undergraduate and graduate students in robotics and AI
- Researchers transitioning from digital AI to embodied systems
- Engineers working on robotics and automation
- Developers interested in AI-robotics integration

## Book Objectives
1. Introduce the concept of Physical AI and embodied intelligence
2. Provide hands-on experience with ROS 2, NVIDIA Isaac, and simulation environments
3. Demonstrate practical integration of Vision-Language-Action systems
4. Prepare readers for advanced robotics development and research

## Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of Python programming
- Familiarity with linear algebra and calculus
- Introductory knowledge of machine learning concepts
- Basic understanding of physics and mechanics

## Technical Requirements
- RTX 4070 or higher GPU recommended for simulation
- Ubuntu 22.04 LTS or Windows 10/11 with WSL2
- At least 32GB RAM for complex simulations
- Jetson Orin Nano for edge deployment examples

## Technical Stack
- **Simulation Environment:** Gazebo, Isaac Sim, Unity
- **Robot Middleware:** ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill with rclpy
- **AI Framework:** NVIDIA Isaac ROS, Omniverse, PyTorch
- **Development Tools:** VS Code, Docker, Git
- **Documentation:** Docusaurus with MDX support

## Pedagogical Approach
- Theory supported by practical examples
- Step-by-step tutorials with code
- Simulation-first approach with real robot deployment
- Exercises and projects at the end of each chapter
- Multi-lingual support (English and Urdu)

## Assessment Strategy
- Chapter-end quizzes with conceptual questions
- Programming assignments with simulation environments
- Project-based assessments with increasing complexity
- Peer review and collaboration exercises

## Success Metrics
- Students can implement basic robot control systems
- Students can integrate AI models with robotic platforms
- Students can deploy vision-language-action systems
- Students can design and execute robotic projects
